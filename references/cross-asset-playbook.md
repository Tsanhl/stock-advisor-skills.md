# Cross-Asset Swing Playbook

Use this playbook to combine indicators instead of relying on a single signal.

## Core Indicators
- Dollar direction (DXY)
- US yield direction (2Y/10Y and real yields when available)
- Oil direction (WTI/Brent)
- Gold and silver direction
- Equity index risk tone (SPY/QQQ/Russell)
- Credit-risk tone (spreads if available)

## Combination Patterns

### Pattern A
- Conditions: `USD up + yields up + gold/silver weak`
- Typical implication: Headwind for precious metals and duration-sensitive growth equities.
- Sector tilt: Financials may hold better than high-duration tech.

### Pattern B
- Conditions: `USD down + yields down + gold/silver strong`
- Typical implication: Tailwind for metals/miners and rate-sensitive risk assets.
- Sector tilt: Growth and precious-metals complex can outperform.

### Pattern C
- Conditions: `Oil up sharply + inflation concern rising`
- Typical implication: Positive for upstream energy; negative for transports and cost-sensitive industries.
- Sector tilt: Energy up, consumer discretionary/industrials can face margin pressure.

### Pattern D
- Conditions: `Equities up but credit stress widening`
- Typical implication: Rally quality is weaker; higher pullback risk.
- Sector tilt: Prefer quality/defensive names over speculative beta.

## Conflict Handling
If indicators conflict:
- Reduce confidence.
- Shorten horizon.
- Tighten risk controls.
- State exactly which indicators disagree.

## Professional Framing Rule
Always state:
- What is observed (fact)
- What it may imply (inference)
- What would invalidate the view
