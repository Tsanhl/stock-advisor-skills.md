# Research Quality Checklist

Use this checklist before sending the final answer.

## A. Scope Fit

- Research question is clearly answered.
- Output depth and format match user request.
- User-specified domain is captured and reflected in source selection.
- Geographic and time scope are explicit.

## B. Evidence Strength

- Every key claim has a source.
- Critical claims are cross-checked with independent sources where possible.
- Source quality is strong (primary or highly reputable secondary).

## C. Freshness and Date Integrity

- "Latest/current" claims were verified with recently updated sources.
- Event dates and publication dates are not conflated.
- Relative date language is converted to exact dates where useful.
- Final delta rescan was run close to output time for time-sensitive topics.
- If no new items were found in final rescan, report states that explicitly.

## D. Numerical and Method Consistency

- Units, currencies, and base years are consistent or clearly converted.
- Totals, percentages, and trends are internally consistent.
- Methodology caveats that affect interpretation are noted.

## E. Reasoning Quality

- Claims follow from evidence without jumps.
- Assumptions are explicit.
- Uncertainty and competing interpretations are acknowledged.

## F. Writing Quality

- Executive summary reflects body findings accurately.
- Section flow is coherent and non-repetitive.
- Language is precise, fluent, and free of ambiguity.
- No vague channel wording (for example "breadth cooled") without specifying breadth of what and with which metric.
- No unexplained mechanism statements; each mechanism states direction, comparator, and impact horizon.

## F.1 Indicator Block Clarity

- Indicator section uses numbered cards (`Indicator 1`, `Indicator 2`, ...), not repeated anonymous `Indicator:` labels.
- Each indicator includes `Current`, `Prior`, and `Baseline` with exact dates and units when available.
- Each indicator includes one explicit interpretation sentence and one "why it matters now" sentence.
- Each indicator has at least one direct source citation using inline `[n]`.
- Indicator cards do not use standalone `Source:` lines; citation mapping is handled by inline `[n]` + final `References`.

## G. Transparency

- Missing data and evidence limitations are clearly disclosed.
- Conclusions are bounded to what sources support.
- Single-source dependency is disclosed for any major claim lacking independent confirmation.

## H. Insight Value (Premium Bar)

- Report identifies what is non-obvious versus common narrative.
- At least one quantified implication is provided for each major insight.
- Decision relevance is explicit (what action, for whom, and when).
- Scenario triggers are specified (what evidence would change the view).

## I. Citation Format Compliance

- Non-trivial factual sentences include inline numeric citations (for example: `[1]`).
- Citation marker placement is directly after relevant sentence or clause.
- Numbering is consistent and reused for the same source.
- Final section is titled `References` and lists numbered entries matching all inline markers.

## J. Independent Judgment and Coverage

- Report does not blindly follow the user's outline when it is incomplete.
- Missing but decision-critical sections are added (counter-case, risk triggers, limitations).
- User assertions are validated before being accepted as facts.
